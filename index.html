Methodology

My product methodology is simple: focus on solving problems that create a lot of value for your stakeholders and involve as many people in the process as possible. Within this vain, it's important to build as many feedback loops into your process whilst keeping it simple.

In terms of the actual process, I take a design-thinking approach with the user or customer at the center. The process at Alchemist had five phases: 1) Planning, 2) Researching and Defining, 3) Designing and Documenting, 4) Developing and Testing, and 5) Marketing and Measuring. These phases made sense and worked for our team, but in other cases, it doesn't work. Other teams need more or less phases depending on size of company, type of environment (remote or not) and other factors. I can go into more detail about these specific phases, but the key is building feedback loops into your process.

Services

Product Management

The key to an efficient and sustainable product team is setting a clear strategy and process. In my experience, I have seen companies without product strategy, or an outdated product strategy. A product strategy guides almost every decision. I can help you create your first iteration and define your strategy now and will revisit it every year.

Process is often looked down upon in startups and might not be necessary with two people. When you're team grows to four, five or more, things start falling through the cracks. I can help you make a basic process, also helping new team members get up-to-speed faster.

UX/UI Design

Today, every product should be a great experience. I can help set up a research process to map out user journeys. Beyond improving the overall product experience, this phase improves the hand over to developers and makes the testing process much easier.

I also learned interface design at Alchemist. If you don't have an internal design team, I can turn these user journeys into high fidelity designs to get preliminary feedback before any code is written.

Front End Web Development

The final step is creating the actual interfaces. My recent course covered HTML, CSS, and Vanilla JS within the context of a modular RESTful application. I'm currently in the process of learning React and AngularJS . While I'm still new to front end development, I learn fast and will put in the additional time to produce the best possible product for you.
